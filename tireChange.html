How to Change a Flat Tire

  1. Consider the parts you will need for this job and make a list. Needed items: Jack (Consult the user manual to find location in vehicle or use aftermarket), Tire Iron, Jack Stands (Recommended, not necessary) and of course, a tire (or the donut). .

  2. Safety precautions: Make sure wheel is straight and emergency/parking brake is engaged. (Wheel should not be like the one in the picture)
  3. Loosen lug nuts With the tire iron, (choose the right side), and loosen the bolts on the wheels, just enough to spin them with your hand.
  4. Jack up car Take a look in your car's user manual and find the proper location to jack up your car. Place the jack under your car and pump it high enough to where the wheel is about 2 feet off the ground.
  5. Finish removing the lug nuts/wheel. Using your fingers or tire iron, remove the rest of the nuts and then the wheel.
  6. Put new wheel on. Take the new wheel (or donut) and align it with the studs so it can slide right on.
  7. Install lug nuts. When installing the lug nuts, refer to the diagram below on how to install properly and tighten until the wheel starts to spins.
  8. Lower car. Using the jack, lower the car until you can be able to move the jack from under the car.
  9. Fully tighten the lug nuts. Since the wheel was in the air, it was not able to be fully tightened. Now that the wheel is on the ground, tighten the nuts until it feels snug on the stud.
  10. You're finished!. Now that you have properly changed your flat tire, give yourself a pat on the back for a job well done.
